04 — Research

QUITEL 2025

Graph Similarity for
Influenza Inhibitors

Presented at the Congress of Theoretical Chemists of Latin Expression (QUITEL 2025) — one of the most important computational chemistry gatherings in Latin America. The research applies graph similarity algorithms to identify molecules and proteins capable of inhibiting influenza.

This work is part of the Universidad de los Andes project, funded by the chemist association, and developed in collaboration with the Doctorate in Computational Chemistry at Universidad de los Andes — where mathematicians and chemists meet to build smarter, faster discovery pipelines.

The core insight: if two molecules share a similar graph structure, they likely share functional behavior. We leverage that to reduce the search space for potential inhibitors dramatically.

05 — Case Study

Bayesian Results

What does the model
actually tell us about tourists?

A Bayesian Hierarchical Logistic Regression built with PyMC to predict premium accommodation choice in the Canary Islands. The key insight of the hierarchical approach: each country of residence gets its own learned intercept — revealing deep patterns in how nationalities approach travel spending, without overfitting to small groups.

Key Findings
01
Northern Europeans are the
premium segment
Sweden, the Netherlands, and Norway show posterior probabilities of premium accommodation 40–60% above the global average. Their travel behavior signals that the Canary Islands is a planned, deliberate luxury destination for Nordic visitors. Hospitality marketing for these markets should emphasize quality, exclusivity, and premium amenities.
🇸🇪 Suecia +0.63 🇳🇱 Países Bajos +0.57 🇳🇴 Noruega +0.52 🇬🇧 UK +0.48
02
Domestic tourists
prefer budget stays
Spain (−0.38) — the host country itself — has the most negative intercept in the model. Even controlling for income and trip purpose, domestic and nearby tourists (Portugal −0.22, Italy −0.15) consistently choose lower-category accommodation. Proximity reduces the perceived value of premium lodging.
🇪🇸 España −0.38 🇵🇹 Portugal −0.22 🇮🇹 Italia −0.15
03
Trip investment
amplifies the effect
Across all nationalities, tourists who spend more on accommodation and stay more nights overwhelmingly select premium categories. The model captures a self-reinforcing loop: longer trips → higher nightly budgets → premium selection. Meanwhile, price sensitivity pushes in the opposite direction.
04
Digital channels
predict quality seekers
Tourists who discovered the destination via internet and social media show measurably higher premium propensity. This signals an emerging digitally-informed segment actively seeking quality — a high-value target for hospitality brands investing in social content.
